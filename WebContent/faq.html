What is FloodAlert India?

FloodAlert India is a national flood monitoring and early warning system developed by the Ministry of Jal Shakti in collaboration with IMD and State Disaster Management Authorities. Our system provides real-time flood alerts, preparedness information, and emergency response coordination to help protect communities across India from flood disasters.

How do I receive flood alerts?

You can receive flood alerts through multiple channels:

  • Registered users get alerts via SMS and email
  • Mobile app notifications (available on Android and iOS)
  • Website dashboard with real-time updates
  • Local authorities may also broadcast alerts through sirens or loudspeakers in high-risk areas

Ensure your contact information is up-to-date in your account settings to receive alerts.

What do the different alert levels mean?

Our alert system uses three severity levels:

  • Red (High): Immediate danger - evacuate if advised or move to higher ground
  • Orange (Medium): Significant flooding expected - prepare to evacuate
  • Yellow (Low): Potential flooding - stay alert and monitor updates

Each alert will include specific instructions based on your location and the flood risk.

How do I register for FloodAlert India?

Registration is simple and free:

  1. Visit our website or download the mobile app
  2. Click on "Register" or "Sign Up"
  3. Provide your name, address, contact information, and email
  4. Create a password
  5. Verify your email address through the link we send you

Once registered, you can customize your alert preferences in your account settings.

I forgot my password. How can I reset it?

To reset your password:

  1. Go to the login page
  2. Click on "Forgot Password"
  3. Enter the email address associated with your account
  4. Check your email for a password reset link (valid for 24 hours)
  5. Follow the instructions to create a new password

If you don't receive the email, please check your spam folder or contact our support team.

How can I report a flood incident?

You can report flood incidents through:

  • Mobile App: Use the "Report Incident" feature with photos and location
  • Website: Log in to your dashboard and submit a report
  • SMS: Send details to our emergency number (XXXX-XXXXXX)
  • Phone: Call the National Disaster Response Force at 1078

Please provide as much detail as possible, including location, water level, and any immediate dangers.

What information should I include when reporting a flood?

When reporting a flood, please include:

  • Exact location (address or landmarks)
  • Current water level (ankle-deep, knee-deep, etc.)
  • Rate of water rise (slow, medium, fast)
  • Any visible damage or blocked roads
  • Photos if possible (showing water level and surroundings)
  • Your contact information for follow-up

This information helps authorities assess the situation and respond appropriately.

What should I do when I receive a flood alert?

When you receive a flood alert:

  1. Read carefully: Note the severity level and specific instructions
  2. Prepare to evacuate: Gather essential items (medicines, documents, food)
  3. Move to safety: If advised to evacuate, go to higher ground or designated shelters
  4. Avoid water: Don't walk or drive through floodwaters
  5. Stay informed: Monitor updates through official channels
  6. Help others: Check on neighbors, especially elderly or disabled

Always follow instructions from local authorities.

How can I prepare my family for floods?

Flood preparedness checklist:

  • Know your risk: Check if you live in a flood-prone area
  • Emergency kit: Prepare with food, water, medicines for 3 days
  • Important documents: Keep copies in waterproof containers
  • Family plan: Decide meeting points and communication methods
  • Home preparation: Learn how to turn off utilities, elevate valuables
  • Stay informed: Register with FloodAlert India and monitor weather

Visit our "Flood Preparedness" section for detailed guides and checklists.
