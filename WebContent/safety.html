Flood Safety Guidelines

EMERGENCY ALERT: If flash flooding is occurring, move immediately to higher ground. Do not wait for instructions!

Before a Flood (Preparation)

Avoid building in flood prone areas unless you elevate and reinforce your home.
Elevate the furnace, water heater, and electric panel if susceptible to flooding.
Install "Check Valves" in sewer traps to prevent floodwater from backing up into drains.
Contact community officials about flood barriers (levees, beams, floodwalls) in your area.
Seal basement walls with waterproofing compounds to avoid seepage.

When a Flood is Likely

Listen to the radio or television for official information and updates.
Be aware that flash flooding can occur suddenly with little warning.
Move immediately to higher ground if flash flooding is possible - don't wait for instructions.
Know your area's flood risks - streams, drainage channels, canyons, etc.
Don't ignore evacuation orders - leave immediately when advised.

Preparing to Evacuate

Secure your home - bring in outdoor furniture if time permits.
Move essential items to an upper floor.
Turn off utilities at main switches/valves if instructed to do so.
Disconnect electrical appliances before flooding occurs.
Never touch electrical equipment if you're wet or standing in water.

Evacuation Safety

Don't walk through moving water - just 6 inches can knock you down.
If you must walk in water, choose areas where water isn't moving.
Use a stick to check ground firmness ahead of you.
Never drive into flooded areas - "Turn Around Don't Drown"
If floodwaters rise around your car, abandon it for higher ground if safe.
Don't attempt to drive through floodwaters - your car can be swept away.

Remember: Just 12 inches of rushing water can carry away most cars, and just 2 feet of water can sweep away SUVs and trucks. Never underestimate the power of floodwaters.

Emergency Contacts

National Disaster Helpline: 112
Police: 100
Keep local emergency numbers handy (see our Emergency Contacts page)
Listen to weather alerts on radio/TV for official instructions
